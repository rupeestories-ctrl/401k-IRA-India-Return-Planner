Assumptions & limitations
·US progressive tax used throughout: usTax(income + withdrawal) − usTax(income)
·State tax added as a flat rate on taxable income during US-resident years only
·US withholding rate shown for cash flow timing only — not used as final tax in any calculation
·FTC: US progressive tax credited against India gross tax (not flat withholding)
·All 401k / IRA withdrawals treated as ordinary income in India (simplification — actual classification may vary)
·Roth conversion taxes assumed paid from external cash — paying from the account itself reduces net outcome
·Pre-return conversion % is user-controlled; optimal split depends on bracket headroom each year
·India new tax regime FY25 + 4% cess; surcharge not included — at high withdrawal amounts (above ₹50L) surcharge (10–37%) may apply
·Section 89A modelled as 20-year spread (approximation); actual relief depends on contribution history
·RNOR mid-point used as withdrawal approximation — actual tax depends on remittance timing and control conditions
·Roth India treatment unclear — this planner assumes US tax-free; India may tax gains for ROR residents
·10% early withdrawal penalty shown; exceptions exist (Rule 72(t) SEPP, disability, Roth basis)
·RMDs use IRS Uniform Lifetime Table approximation, age 73–87
·For planning purposes only — not tax or legal advice. Consult a cross-border CPA / CA.
