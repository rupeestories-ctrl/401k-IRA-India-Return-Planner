🗽 US Citizens: India-only treaty position is not available to you
The DTAA Savings Clause (Article 1, para 4) lets the US tax its citizens as if the treaty didn't exist. You will owe US tax on 401k/IRA income regardless of where you live. The right tool is the Foreign Tax Credit (Form 67 in India) — not a treaty workaround. Any tool or advisor suggesting "India-only" treatment for a USC should be questioned.
🚨 Exit tax risk — get CPA advice before surrendering your Green Card
You've held your GC for 8+ years. If you're a "covered expatriate" (net worth > $2M or avg annual tax liability > ~$201K), the IRS treats all assets — including your 401k balance — as distributed on the day you abandon. This can be the largest single tax event of your return. File Form 8854. Get a cross-border CPA to model this before you act.

Your action plan

FATCA & CRS: Your custodian files a 1099-R for every distribution regardless of where you live. India participates in CRS — non-disclosure of foreign income carries real risk. Once you're ROR, file Schedule FA (foreign assets) and Schedule FSI (foreign income) with your India ITR. Penalty for missing this: ₹10 lakh under the Black Money Act.
